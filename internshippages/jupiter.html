Jupiter Research Foundation Microscope Payload V1.0

Jupiter Research Foundation desired to build an automated microscope payload to deploy in a standard Liquid Robotics Drybox.

We chose to build our own microscope out of Navitar microscope components, building a exoskeleton frame to align all the parts to image through a simple rectangular glass flowcell.

The first rotational flow cell concept. Complex and big. When I arrived at Jupiter Research Foundation, this was the extent of the microscope project.

After considering the optics of the desired objective and condenser pair, I did a little more research and found that for our field of view and depth of focus combination, there existed an off-the-shelf borosilicate glass tube that matched our optical parameters. We brought some in for testing and they worked great!

We liked the optical qualities of the flow cell, but obviously our original version wasn't going to cut it. We build a standard microscope-slide mountable version, so that we could continue to use our bench microscope, but with more a much more stable assembly.

After much CAD iteration, and a few prototypes, this was our final flow cell mount. With good access for the objective and condenser, and a good rigid mount, we are ready to build the rest of the microscope.
