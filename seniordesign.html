Washington University in St. Louis Senior Design Project

Our design problem was to create a solar radiation tracking device. The primary goal of this device was to keep a solar panel perpendicular to the solar radiation with 95% efficiency throughout the day.

Initial concept drawing of a counterweighted panel. First Solidworks CAD to flush out the concepts and ensure reasonable sizes.
Flow cell being tested in a more permanent mount. The final production ready for mount into the microscope.
Test exoskeleton made from 3D printed parts. Test fit microscope components.
Fully assembled microscope ready for testing.
