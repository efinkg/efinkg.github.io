Washington University in St. Louis Senior Design Project

Our design problem was to create a solar radiation tracking device. The primary goal of this device was to keep a solar panel perpendicular to the solar radiation with 95% efficiency throughout the day. We were able to build a sun tracking panel, untethered from an eletctrical grid, that charged the battery faster than it was run down.

Initial concept drawing of a counterweighted panel. First Solidworks CAD to flush out the concepts and ensure reasonable sizes.

From here, we worked on ensuring that we could consistenly interface our photosensors with the Arduino embedded computer, and use the sensor readings to move the panel towards the sun.
