Project Background: In Spring of 2015 I was taking courses in Robotics Dynamics and Control and Robotics Lab, and I wanted to get a chance to practice my skills in design, component selection, and programming robotic systems. I teamed up with Max Kapcznski who I had worked with on my senior design project and we started designing a robot arm. Our goal is to pick up a 12oz can a placed anywhere in reach of the arm.

First Solidworks CAD to flush out the concepts and ensure reasonable sizes. Click here for a 3D view of this CAD. With appreciation to Max Kapczynski for his CAD work.

At this point it made sense to build a MATLAB simulated model of the robot arm to ensure that we would be able to have all of the degrees of freedom we needed in order to pick up our beverage can from any location or orientation in reach of the base.

Before we could dig deeper into component selection, the electronics system flow had to be well defined. The easiest way of doing this was to build an electronics layout map and be sure that every signal we wanted was diagrammed.

Updated Solidworks CAD to replace "reasonable size" components with actual CAD models of servos and mechanical parts that can be turned into drawings to machine parts from. Click here for a 3D view of this CAD. At this point, the CAD is ~95% done, and can be used for center of mass, simulations for structural integrity, and modelling static and dynamic forces to be sure the arm is sufficiently structural. We found some issues to rework.

Following the calculations done on the robot arm model, we felt the need to strengthen the upper arm at least, to build a triangle structure rather than a flat plate. This would hopefully greatly improve side-to-side rigidity.
